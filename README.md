# CS-250-Retrospective

1.How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?

    User needs are processed through the lens of what the customer wants to do. The customer likely does not have technical knowledge 
    of developing software, and so inquiring as to what they want the software to do would be more accessible. 
    The structure of that statement is also important, as it must detail the stakeholder, what they want to do, and for what 
    reasons. This is because the developer needs to know who they are cattering to, what they want to do, and what they want to 
    achieve, respectively. 
        -Knowing the specific stakeholder(s) who posed the request provides helpful context that can be put toward more accurately 
            appealing to their needs. 
        -Having a description of the action that the software needs to perform is essential. The customer will request the product 
            to do X, Y, and Z, so that the developer can design the logistics of how the product can do those tasks.
        -Knowing what they want to achieve is also important context. There may be nuance lost in the description of what the software 
            needs to do, and so understanding the underlying reason can make sure the features are implemented more faithfully to the 
            customer's desires.
    The creation of user stories is important, but upkeeping those stories is equally so. The stakeholders wishes or environment could  
    shift, or more clarity regarding those wishes/environment could be revealed. Continuous reflection ensures that the product will 
    slowly manifest into exactly what stakeholders want. 

2.How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

    While it may not be commonly seen as such, I would describe software development as similar to an art. An artist, upon recieving 
    a commission, will stand in front of an empty canvas and paint according to what the petiotioner wants. With every painting, 
    sculpture, or artwork, the artist becomes better at and more knowledgeable of their craft. I'm sure that my early software will 
    be unrefined, but with a commitment to reflection and growth, I will improve. This principle of improvement is also core to 
    each project. An artist can take their sketches to their client for feedback, then take that feedback to improve the painting. 
    As time passes, the product will develop into exactly what the customer envisioned. 

    Another principle I hope to employ is flexibility. The potential for software is endless, but that means that the range of approaches 
    is vast. While software is nice, I can recognize that software exists to fix a certain problem. Software development is, at its 
    core, a question of problem solving, where the developer is tasked with shaping the best tool for the job. This requires an adaptable 
    perspective that can conform to the specific situation. 

3.What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

    Development is typically a collaborative effort, and so working with others well is essential to success. 

    There are some things that are important in any team setting. A cooperative and polite mindset are always
    beneficial. Being positive and constructive will help support teammates, and the reciprocation of those
    actions will generate a comfortable environment for everyone.

    One thing to keep in mind when working in a team, specifically in software development, is the benefits
    of team based problem solving. Having a diverse group with different perspectives can help illuminate 
    different paths to success. Having a firm understanding of the concepts behind development, as well as
    a vibrant and varied outlook will enatil having more insight to offer to the team.
